The time it takes to learn Rust depends on your existing programming background, learning intensity, and the depth of understanding you aim for. Here's a rough estimate based on your dedication:

### **Assumptions**:
- You’re giving **2 to 3 hours of practice daily**.
- Prior programming experience (e.g., in languages like C, C++, or Python) can accelerate learning.

#### **Rust Learning Timeline**:
1. **Beginner Level** (Basic Syntax, Ownership, and Borrowing):
   - **3 to 6 months**: For someone with programming experience, you could expect to be comfortable with Rust basics like ownership, types, functions, and error handling.

2. **Intermediate Level** (Async, Concurrency, Traits, Generics, and Structs):
   - **6 to 12 months**: Developing familiarity with Rust’s advanced features like async programming, iterators, traits, lifetimes, and pattern matching.

3. **Advanced Level** (Systems Programming, Unsafe Code, Concurrency, and FFI):
   - **1 to 2 years**: For deeper system-level programming and using Rust effectively in real-world projects like OS development, web assembly, or embedded systems.

#### **Daily Practice Impact**:
- **2 to 3 hours daily** is an excellent pace for consistent learning.
- **Consistency** and **practice** play a key role in gaining fluency.
- **Real-world projects** (like building a basic OS) can significantly enhance your learning.

### Final Notes:
- Everyone’s pace may vary depending on background and dedication.
- Practical projects (like working on Rust-based OS development) can speed up comprehension and retention.
- Consider contributing to open-source projects or participating in Rust communities for additional learning resources.
